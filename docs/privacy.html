FRED Security

Privacy Policy

Effective date: March 7, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what data FRED (Fraud Recognition & Easy Detection) collects, how it is used, and your choices. FRED is operated by Skylar Bolton ("we", "us", "our").

We believe in transparency. FRED is open source — you can verify everything described here by reading the code.

1. What Data FRED Collects

FRED is designed to minimize data collection. Here is a complete list of data we handle:

Data Stored Where Purpose
Device ID (random UUID) Your device + our proxy server Rate limiting for free tier
Monthly usage count Your device + our proxy server Enforce check limits
License key (if Premium) Your device + our proxy server Verify subscription status
OpenAI API key (if BYOK) Your device only Authenticate with OpenAI
Analysis history (last 20) Your device only Let you review past checks
Settings (model, theme, etc.) Your device only Remember your preferences

2. What FRED Does NOT Collect

  • We do not collect your name, email address, or any personal information.
  • We do not use analytics, tracking pixels, or cookies.
  • We do not store, log, or retain the content you analyze (emails, text, URLs, or page data).
  • We do not sell or share any data with advertisers or data brokers.

3. How Content Analysis Works

When you use FRED to check content for fraud:

  • Free and Premium users: Your content is sent through our proxy server to OpenAI's API for analysis. Our proxy forwards the request and returns the result — it does not log or store the content.
  • BYOK users: Your content is sent directly from your browser to OpenAI's API. It never touches our servers.

In both cases, OpenAI processes your content to generate the analysis. OpenAI's handling of API data is governed by their API data usage policy, which states that API inputs and outputs are not used to train their models.

4. Data Stored on Our Server

Our proxy server (hosted on Cloudflare Workers) stores only:

  • Rate-limit counters: Your device ID and the number of checks used this month. These automatically expire after 60 days.
  • License validation cache: Whether your license key is valid. This cache expires after 2 hours.

No content, personal information, or browsing history is stored on our server.

5. Third-Party Services

Service What They Receive Their Privacy Policy
OpenAI Content you analyze (emails, text, URLs, page data) openai.com/policies/privacy-policy
LemonSqueezy Payment info (if you subscribe to Premium) lemonsqueezy.com/privacy
Cloudflare Hosts our proxy server (sees request metadata only) cloudflare.com/privacypolicy

6. Your Choices and Rights

  • Delete local data: You can clear your analysis history, API key, and license key from FRED's settings at any time.
  • Uninstall: Removing the FRED extension deletes all locally stored data.
  • Cancel subscription: You can cancel your Premium subscription at any time through LemonSqueezy. Your license key will remain active until the end of the billing period.
  • Data deletion request: To request deletion of your device ID and usage data from our proxy server, email info@fredsecurity.com.

7. Children's Privacy

FRED is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.

8. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by updating the effective date above.

9. Contact

If you have questions about this Privacy Policy, contact us at info@fredsecurity.com.
